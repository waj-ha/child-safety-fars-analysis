
# Ethical Considerations
## Data Privacy

The FARS dataset is publicly available and does not contain personally identifiable information (PII). Individual victims, drivers, and families cannot be identified from the analysis.

## Responsible Use of Data

The purpose of this project is to improve understanding of child safety in road accidents and support evidence-based decision-making. The analysis is not intended to assign blame to individuals or groups.

## Data Quality and Limitations

The dataset may contain missing, incomplete, or unknown values. Results should be interpreted with these limitations in mind. Conclusions are based on recorded data and may not capture all contributing factors.

## Avoiding Bias

Care was taken to present findings objectively without discrimination based on age, gender, race, geographic location, or socioeconomic status. Statistical findings are reported without making assumptions about individuals.

## Transparency

All data cleaning, preprocessing, and analysis steps are documented to ensure reproducibility and transparency. Any assumptions made during the analysis are clearly stated.

## Public Benefit

The analysis aims to contribute to improved child passenger safety, increased awareness of proper restraint use, and support for policies that reduce fatalities and injuries among children.

## Compliance

The project uses publicly available government data from the FARS program and complies with applicable data-use policies and ethical standards for research and data analysis.
